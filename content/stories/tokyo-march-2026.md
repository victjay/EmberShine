Arrived late into Haneda. The city was cold and quiet in a way that only exists between winter and spring — that specific grey-blue light just before something blooms.

## Shimokitazawa

The vintage shops were still closed when I got there in the morning. I had coffee at a tiny place with three seats and a handwritten menu. The owner didn't speak English. I pointed at the espresso and she nodded.

By noon the neighborhood filled with students and record shop browsers. I found a copy of a Fishmans album for 800 yen.

## Yanaka Cemetery

One of those places that doesn't feel like it belongs to the city around it. Cats sleep on the graves. Old women sweep paths. The light comes through the trees at an angle that makes everything look like a film still.

## Shinjuku Backstreets

Golden Gai at 10pm is loud and cramped and perfect. I ended up at a bar with six seats, a bartender who only played Joy Division, and a conversation with a programmer from Osaka who was visiting for a job interview.

He didn't get the job. We had another drink anyway.
